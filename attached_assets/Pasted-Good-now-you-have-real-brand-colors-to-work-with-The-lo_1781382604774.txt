My honest assessment of the current white + gold theme vs logo colors:
The gold theme looks good in isolation but creates a brand conflict — your logo has zero gold in it. When someone sees the logo and then sees a gold-accented page, it feels disconnected. Government clients especially notice inconsistency.

Recommended: Align website to logo colors
--bg:          #FFFFFF   clean white base
--bg-alt:      #F2F4F8   very light blue-gray for alternating sections
--ink:         #0F1E36   near-black navy — headlines, primary text
--ink-soft:    #4A5E78   secondary text
--accent:      #CC2929   red — primary CTAs, active states, violation tags
--accent-navy: #1B3A6B   deep navy — secondary buttons, icons, dark sections
--line:        #DDE3ED   borders on light
--signal:      #2A7A5A   green — "LIVE", "ACTIVE" status only
Why this works:
The navy + red + white is actually a powerful government palette — it's what police departments, national highway authorities, and civic bodies already use. It reads as authority instantly. Red for "alert/action" (violations, CTAs), navy for "trust/institution" (structure, dark panels), white for clarity.
