 Kaaval AI — Government/Smart City Website
Brand & Visual Identity
Build a premium, trustworthy website for Kaaval AI, an AI-based traffic enforcement platform (helmet violation detection + ANPR) that upgrades existing CCTV/ANPR cameras for police departments and smart city authorities. The tone is "Smart City master plan meets government annual report" — confident, institutional, calm. Not a startup demo.
Color System
--ink:        #0A1628   primary dark background (deep navy, never pure black)
--ink-2:      #0F2138   panel/card background
--ink-3:      #16304F   elevated card surfaces
--ink-line:   #1F3A5C   borders / grid lines on dark
--paper:      #F7F8FA   light section background
--paper-2:    #FFFFFF   card background on light sections
--gold:       #C9A24A   primary accent — buttons, highlights, key numbers
--gold-soft:  #E6D2A0   secondary gold — icons, labels on dark
--text-light: #E7ECF2   body text on dark navy (not pure white)
--text-muted: #8FA3B8   secondary text on dark
--steel:      #5C7187   secondary text on light
--signal:     #3FA796   status indicators ("LIVE", "ACTIVE") — muted teal-green
Gold is the only accent color for interactive elements. No neon, no electric blue/cyan, no glowing borders.
Typography

Display/headings: Fraunces (serif, weights 500–900) — gives gravitas, used for all headlines
Body: Inter — clean, modern, highly legible
Labels/data/eyebrows: IBM Plex Mono — small caps, wide letter-spacing, used for section labels, stats, timestamps

Signature Background Pattern
A subtle animated grid covers dark sections: thin lines in --ink-line at 8–12% opacity over --ink, with intersection points that softly pulse on a 60–90s loop (like a live monitoring grid breathing). Never distracting — ambient only.

Page Structure
1. Navigation

Transparent/dark sticky bar. Left: shield icon + "KAAVAL AI" wordmark in gold. Right: "Our Impact" link + gold "Request Pilot Deployment" button.
2. Hero (full viewport height)

Grid-pattern background as above
Gold-bordered pill badge: "SMART CITY INITIATIVE" (mono font, wide letter-spacing)
Large serif headline with rotating text (600ms crossfade every 4s): "The Zero Accident Initiative" → "The Safe City Initiative" → "The Intelligent Enforcement Initiative"
Supporting paragraph: aiming for accident-free roads by transforming existing infrastructure into proactive safety networks
Stat callout card with gold left border: large gold serif number + supporting line (use a real, sourced road-safety statistic — e.g. helmet-related fatality data — framed as why this matters, not as Kaaval's own results)
Two CTAs: gold "Request Pilot Deployment" (filled) + "View Case Study" (ghost/outline)
Right side: Kaaval Command Center panel — dark card with teal "LIVE" indicator, header "KAAVAL COMMAND CENTER", 2×2 grid of camera feed tiles labeled with junction names (e.g. "Junction 01 – Market St"). Each tile shows a faint line-art road/junction illustration in steel-blue. Footer row: "AI PROCESSING: 4 FEEDS · DETECTION RATE: 99.2% · LAST ALERT: 2s AGO"

3. Trust Bar

Horizontal row of institutional badges (gold-bordered pills): "Police Department Pilot" · "Smart City Ready" · "Existing CCTV Compatible" · "Rapid Deployment"
4. How It Works / Live Detection Demo

Light section. Title: "From Camera Feed to Enforcement Action." Show 4–5 camera feed cards (line-art junction illustrations). On hover, overlay AI detection elements appear: bounding boxes around a motorcycle, a "No Helmet" tag, and a number plate crop with extracted text — demonstrating the pipeline visually without needing real footage.
5. Camera Modernization (Comparison)

Two-column comparison, dark section: "Traditional Approach" (replace cameras, high capital cost, long deployment, limited scale) vs "Kaaval AI" (reuse existing cameras, lower investment, fast activation, easily scalable). Gold border highlights the Kaaval column.
6. Product Tiers

Light section. Title: "Choose the Right Tier for Your Junction." 3–4 cards side by side, each representing a deployment tier (e.g. Essential — helmet detection only; Standard — helmet + ANPR; Advanced — full violation suite + analytics dashboard; Enterprise — multi-junction district rollout with centralized command center). Each card: tier name, 3–4 capability bullets, and a cost framed as "Starting from ₹X / camera / month" (placeholder values) — emphasize that Kaaval significantly reduces cost versus traditional camera replacement, without specific calculator math. One tier (Standard) visually highlighted as "Most Deployed."
7. Case Study

Dark section. "First Live Deployment — Ramanputhur Junction, Kanyakumari District." Left: line-art illustration of the junction with camera markers. Right: status ("Pilot Operational") + horizontal timeline: Concept → Prototype → Pilot → District Expansion (current stage highlighted in gold).
8. Live Violation Flow

Light section. Interactive 5-step horizontal stepper users can click through: (1) Motorcycle Detected → (2) Violation Identified → (3) Evidence Captured → (4) Alert Logged → (5) Officer Review. Each step shows a simple illustration + short description, with the active step highlighted in gold.
9. Built For

Dark section, title "Built for Public Safety Institutions." 5 cards: Police Departments, District Administrations, Smart Cities, Highway Authorities, Municipal Corporations — each with an icon and one-line description.
10. By the Numbers

Light section. 3–4 large animated counters on scroll (mono font, gold numbers): contextual road-safety stats (sourced, e.g. helmet non-use fatality rates), "24/7 Monitoring Capability", "1 Unified Platform" — framed as context/motivation, not unverified claims about Kaaval's results.
11. Footer CTA

Full-width dark section with grid pattern. Large serif headline: "Imagine a District With Zero Preventable Road Fatalities." Single gold CTA: "Request Pilot Deployment."
12. Footer

Minimal: logo, contact email, location, links to Privacy/Contact.

Motion & Interaction

Scroll-triggered fade/slide-up reveals, staggered for grouped cards
Hover elevation on cards (subtle shadow lift, no glow)
Hero text rotation (described above)
Animated counters trigger once on scroll into view
Respect prefers-reduced-motion

Avoid
Neon/cyberpunk effects, pure black/white, glowing borders, AI buzzword soup, cost calculators, fabricated performance percentages attributed to Kaaval itself.
